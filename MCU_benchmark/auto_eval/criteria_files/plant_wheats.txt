**Task Progress: the key factors/steps for completing the task** 
 - whether the agent has prepared the ground for planting (e.g., tilled the soil)
 - whether the agent has planted the wheat seeds in the prepared soil
 - whether the agent has ensured the wheat seeds have water nearby for growth
 - whether the agent has harvested the grown wheat

**Action Control: whether the agents have unrelated operations of the task, including useless actions and redundancy actions**
 - e.g., digging unnecessary holes, planting seeds in unsuitable locations, or repeatedly breaking and replanting

**Error Recognition and Correction: whether the agent can promptly identify and rectify its mistakes**
 - e.g., whether the agent recognizes incorrectly tilled or watered land and corrects it
 - whether the corrected results demonstrate improvement and reduce flaws in the final product

**Creative Attempts: any creative attempts exhibited by the agent during doing task**
 - e.g., arranging the wheat field in unique patterns or utilizing fences for protection against mobs
 - implementing irrigation systems or using bone meal to accelerate growth

**Task Completion Efficiency**
 - whether the time taken by the agent to complete the task falls within a reasonable range
 - whether effective agricultural strategies were employed to minimize unnecessary repetitions or errors

**Material Selection and Usage: whether the agent correctly utilize the given materials**
 - e.g., using the correct tools for tilling and ensuring the right amount of seeds and water is used
