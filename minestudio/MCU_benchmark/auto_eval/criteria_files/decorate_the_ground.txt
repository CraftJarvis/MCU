**Task Progress: the key factors/steps for completing the task** 
 - whether the agent selects an area of ground to decorate
 - whether the agent places decorative items or blocks on the ground
 - whether the agent covers a significant portion of the ground with decorations

**Action Control: whether the agents have unrelated operations of the task, including useless actions and redundancy actions**
 - e.g. random placement of blocks that do not contribute to the decoration
 - e.g. unnecessary digging or modifying of terrain unrelated to the decoration

**Error Recognition and Correction: whether the agent can promptly identify and rectify its mistakes**
 - e.g. whether the agent recognizes poorly arranged decorations and rearranges them
 - whether the corrected results demonstrate improvement and enhance the overall aesthetic of the ground decoration

**Creative Attempts: any creative attempts exhibited by the agent during doing task**
 - e.g. use of a variety of colors and patterns in the decoration
 - e.g. incorporation of unique elements like pathways or thematic designs

**Task Completion Efficiency**
 - whether the time taken by the agent to complete the task falls within a reasonable range
 - whether effective decoration strategies were employed to minimize unnecessary repetitions or errors

**Material Selection and Usage: whether the agent correctly utilize the given materials**
 - e.g. diverse use of available materials to create visually appealing ground decorations
 - whether the materials were used efficiently without excessive waste
