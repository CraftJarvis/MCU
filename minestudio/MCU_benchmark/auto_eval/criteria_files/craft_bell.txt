**Task Progress: the key factors/steps for completing the task** 
 - whether the agent collects the necessary materials: gold ingots and iron ingots
 - whether the agent accesses the crafting table
 - whether the agent crafts the bell using the correct recipe

**Action Control: whether the agents have unrelated operations of the task, including useless actions and redundancy actions**
 - e.g. wandering without purpose, collecting unrelated materials, excessive opening and closing of inventory or crafting table.

**Error Recognition and Correction: whether the agent can promptly identify and rectify its mistakes**
 - e.g. whether the agent identifies incorrect material placements in the crafting grid and corrects them
 - whether the corrected results demonstrate improvement and reduce flaws in the final product.

**Creative Attempts: any creative attempts exhibited by the agent during doing task**
 - e.g. using alternative methods to gather materials, such as bartering or mining
 - e.g. crafting additional related items or structures as part of the bell crafting process

**Task Completion Efficiency**
 - whether the time taken by the agent to complete the task falls within a reasonable range.
 - whether effective strategies were employed to minimize unnecessary repetitions or errors, such as efficient material gathering.

**Material Selection and Usage: whether the agent correctly utilize the given materials**
 - e.g. whether the agent correctly uses the gold ingots and iron ingots in the crafting process
 - whether the agent avoids using excessive or incorrect materials.
